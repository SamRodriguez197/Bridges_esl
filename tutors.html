Overview

Bridges ESL meets every Saturday to provide free ESL lessons for immigrants and visiting internationals in New Haven who want to improve their written and spoken English. Through small-group tutoring sessions, students gain English proficiency, communication skills, and the confidence needed to pursue employment, education, citizenship, and daily life.

Tutors commit to preparing weekly individualized lesson plans and teaching each Saturday morning (excluding holiday breaks). While occasional absences are understandable, we strongly encourage tutors to build consistent, meaningful relationships with their students.

Teaching Levels

Tutors may request to teach specific levels based on their experience and preferences:

Survival Level
For students with minimal English experience, focusing on essential survival vocabulary and basic communication.
Beginner Level
Focus on basic vocabulary, grammar foundations, and everyday scripts for daily interactions.
Intermediate Low/High
Developing conversational fluency, intermediate grammar concepts, and listening comprehension skills.
Advanced/Conversational
For high proficiency students looking to refine fluency and tackle complex language structures.

Lesson Plans

Each week, tutors work with 1‚Äì3 students (ideally 1:1) and are expected to bring a concrete 2-hour lesson plan tailored to student goals. Lessons often incorporate vocabulary, grammar, listening, and conversation, with time distributed based on individual needs.

Topics may include real-life scenarios like visiting the doctor, navigating banks, ordering food, TOEFL/GRE prep, or academic reading. Lesson plans can be printed for you if emailed to the team before the deadline, or you may bring your own copies.
