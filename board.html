The Board

Meet the dedicated student leaders who guide Bridges ESL and support our community of English learners

2024-2025 Bridges ESL Board
Co-President: Brian Moore
b.moore@yale.edu
Brian is from Chicago and is a Junior in Silliman majoring in humanities. Brian does everything under the sun, but his most rewarding activity besides Bridges ESL is Link New Haven, which operates a free community resource desk in New Haven Green every week. He also loves endless conversations, any sport that involves running and learning new words. Brian is doing Bridges because when his mother migrated from Mexico, she had a difficult time overcoming the language barrier. In high school, teaching his mother English sparked within him a passion for spreading the gift of literacy. He joined Bridges his first semester on campus, and he will continue being involved with the organization until his last. Tutoring for Bridges is always the highlight of his week, and he can't stop telling people about his wonderful experience!

ignacio.ruiz-sanchez@yale.edu
Hailing from Collinsville, VA, Ignacio is a senior neuroscience major with a Chinese Certificate in Ezra Stiles College. Over his four years in Bridges, he has loved seeing the progress and passion for learning among students, as well as being witness to the strong support systems fostered by Yale's community. Ignacio is excited to see both new and returning tutors help welcome students into the family. Outside of Bridges, he enjoys trying new foods, working out whenever possible, hanging out at cute cafes, going for walks in East Rock, and spending time with friends.
Secretary: Yuanyu Chen
yuanyu.chen@yale.edu
Yuanyu is a junior in Branford college majoring in Neuroscience. Yuanyu is from Reading, PA, and is involved in Community Health Educators, neuro research, and interning at Gather Haven. In her free time, she likes learning to bake, going to supermarkets, crocheting, and sketching. The favorite part of Bridges ESL for Yuanyu has been the interactions and cultural exchanges she's had. She loves that she can come into class and hear everyone's wonderful stories about their strange experiences, new coffee spots, and cooking adventures.
Treasurer: Tiffany Zhou
tiffany.zhou@yale.edu
Hailing from New Hampshire, Tiffany is a sophomore majoring in Molecular, Cellular, and Developmental Biology in Silliman College. Growing up with immigrant parents, she experienced firsthand the challenges of learning English and, as such, appreciates the work Bridges does to support immigrants in New Haven. Through Bridges, she has had the opportunity to meet individuals from diverse backgrounds and cultures, something she considers a beautiful aspect of the organization. In addition to her involvement with Bridges, Tiffany is active in several service clubs on campus, including HAPPY, the Red Cross Club, and the Nourish Project. Recently, she has also begun exploring Chinese traditional dance. In her free time, Tiffany enjoys engaging in arts and crafts.
Director of Recruitment: Denise Peng
denise.peng@yale.edu
Denise Peng (she/her) is from Queens, New York and a senior MCDB major in Ezra Stiles college. She has been working with Bridges since her freshman year and is excited for her role as director of recruitment this year. Outside of Bridges, Denise works on the YFAM eboard and is also looking forward to serving as an Academic Strategies mentor. She is super excited to meet everyone, don't hesitate to reach out :)
Co-Director of Education: Anette Diaz
anette.diaz@yale.edu
Originally from New Jersey, Anette is a senior at Ezra Stiles majoring in Latin American studies, with certificates in Spanish and Education Studies. As the daughter of immigrants, she has dedicated much of her organizing work to supporting migrant communities both in the U.S. and abroad. Having been with Bridges for three years, she is continually impressed by the supportive community built within the classes. For her, organizations like this are essential in opening up educational opportunities for local New Haven migrants. In addition to her work with Bridges, she serves as an interpreter for Haven Clinic, is a student assistant for the EDST department, and a choreographer for Ballet Folklorico Mexicano de Yale. If she's ever missing, you'll most likely find her sunbathing at the farm.
Co-Director of Education: Jaime Cha
jungbin.cha@yale.edu
Jaime is a junior in Morse majoring in chemical engineering. Jaime is an international student from Bundang, South Korea. Besides Bridges ESL, Jaime is involved in doing research in the Turner Lab and the Winter Lab, Pitches and Tones A Capella (yayy pitches!!) and is the co-president of the Yale International Korean Club, Hanppuri. Jaime also works in the Marx Library and ULAs for Physics 188. In her free time, Jaime likes to sing, cook, and rewatch Harry Potter. Being an international student from Korea, she also learned English as a second language. She wants to give back by helping fellow English learners because she knows how hard it is to move to a new country and start speaking in a language that you have never used in daily life before. She cherishes being able to forge unique friendships with people from all over the world through Bridges ESL.
Director of Publicity: Asuka Koda
asuka.koda@yale.edu
From New York City, Asuka Koda (she/her) is a sophomore in Davenport double majoring in Math and Philosophy. Asuka was inspired to join Bridges because as an immigrant herself, Asuka wished this was a resource she and her mom had access to when learning English. In addition to her work in Bridges, Asuka is a staff reporter at the Yale Daily News and loves to create pottery in her spare time.
Co-Director of Pairing: Rosa Anderson-Barrera
rosa.andersonbarrera@yale.edu
Rosa (she/her) is a senior in Pierson college majoring in Political Science and Latin American Studies. She hails from Chattanooga, TN with roots in Guatemala. During the school year, you can find Rosa volunteering at Dwight Hall, in the MEChA room with her fellow members, or taking LONG walks around campus. She is a lover of tropical fruits, magical realism, and funky jewelry.
Co-Director of Pairing: Linda Do
linda.do@yale.edu
Linda Do is a sophomore in Pierson studying Molecular Biophysics and Biochemistry. Originally from Fresno, CA. Linda decided to join Bridges because she experienced first-hand the difficulty of trying to learn a new language. Linda, while trying to learn Vietnamese, realized that there weren't many online resources in English available for learning Vietnamese. She was eventually able to learn Vietnamese through conversing with her parents, and realized then how invaluable practicing with native speakers was to improving one's language skills. Linda now wants to help English learners in return and support their language learning journey. Since joining Bridges ESL, she has enjoyed hearing about people's experiences around the world. In her free time, Linda likes to take walks (when it's warm enough outside for a Californian), go down Wikipedia rabbit holes, and think about foods she'd like to try. On campus, Linda is involved in the Vietnamese Student Association (VISA), Southeast Asian Movement (SEAM) and Yale Grammatical Diversity Project (YGDP; I.e., linguistics research on varieties of English spoken in North America).
